About Tushita

Tushita Mahayana Meditation Centre Delhi was established in 1979 under the spiritual guidance of the Founders of FPMT, the late Venerable Lama Thubten Yeshe and the present Spiritual Director, Venerable Lama Thubten Zopa Rinpoche.

The FPMT, Foundation for the Preservation of the Mahayana Tradition, refers to an international network of Dharma centres, projects and services.

Programmes

Teachings and Lectures

We offer regular guided Meditation and Contemplation drop-in sessions both at our physical centre in Hauz Khas, New Delhi as well as on ZOOM. These are on different daily topics, and led by various teachers, mainly our Spiritual Programme Coordinator Kabir Saxena ji.

Mondays, Tuesdays and Thursdays @ 6:30pm IST
Sundays @ 11am IST
(Please check our Event Calendar for updated dates and times)

These sessions are suitable for both beginners as well as more experientially advanced students. They are free/donation-based and open to all.

You do not need to register to attend in-person. Our regular sessions are ongoing and non-sequential, so you may simply drop in at any time, and attend whichever sessions you wish.

Sessions are 1.5 hours with time for Q&A at the end.

Join our official WhatsApp group for regular programme updates!

Morning Meditation Practice

Tushita Centre holds morning meditation sessions, from Mondays to Fridays, both at our physical center as well as on Zoom. Meditation is a practice that helps us slow down from the fast pace of life and tune into what's going on within us. A silent meditation is effective when someone knows how to use their imagination and has a desire to gain new perspectives on themselves and others. Join us for the Silent Meditation and cultivating an awareness of your body in the present and to focus on mindfulness in order to foster calmness, clarity, and acceptance.

Weekly Schedule (all times in IST):
Mondays-Fridays, 6:30am-7am IST - Silent Meditation Practice

Meditation sessions are 30-min long. You are welcome to stay after the session and continue your own practice silently.

Sutra Recitation

Lama Zopa Rinpoche has suggested that all FPMT centers recite the Sanghata Sutra. The recitation of this sutra yields enormous benefits for all those who hear or recite it, and has been recommended by Rinpoche specifically in order to generate the merit that will be needed to bring to completion the Maitreya Project, which itself will yield enormous benefits for countless beings.

1:- Golden Light Sutra
2:- Sanghata Sutra
