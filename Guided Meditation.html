Guided Meditation


Tushita Centre Delhi holds a daily guided meditation practice both at our physical centre as well as on ZOOM. The focus of our morning meditations is on shamatha - calm-abiding meditation using the breath as our object. These sessions are lightly guided. We also supplement our regular shamatha practice with guided vipashyana (insight) meditations every Thursday, on a variety of topics including "Four Immeasurables" and "nature of mind". On Fridays we offer an opportunity to ask questions related to your shamatha practice.

Weekly Schedule (all times in IST):
Mondays-Wednesdays, 8:30am-8:50am - Shamatha practice
Thursdays, 8:30am-8:50am - Guided vipashyana meditation (varying topics)
Fridays, 8:30am-9am - Shamatha practice + Q&A
Meditation sessions are 20-min long.

You are welcome to stay after the session and continue your own practice silently.

TO RECEIVE UPDATES, as well as to receive audio recordings of the Thursday vipashyana guided meditations to practice on your own, please join our WhatsApp group here.

DESCRIPTION: Shamatha - also known as "calm-abiding" or "single-pointedness" - is a meditation practice to cultivate stability and focus of the mind by resting one's attention on a single object. During these sessions, we will focus on the breath as our object. Shamatha is the foundation upon which all else can be built in our practice. Geshe Lhundub Sopa says, "Bodhisattvas strive to achieve shamatha because it has a special benefit. Once you have that mental power, the senses follow the mind." Vipashyana is the cultivation of wisdom, on the stable foundation of shamatha. In these sessions, we will supplement our shamatha practice with weekly guided meditations on topics like the Four Immeasurables, analyzing afflictive emotions, nature of mind, and wisdom of emptiness.
